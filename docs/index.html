What is Gstrl?

Gstrl is a free, open-source macOS app that adds a gesture layer on top of your normal workflow. It uses Apple's Vision framework to track your hand poses at 60fps, giving you another way to interact when a gesture is faster than a click.

Circle something to screenshot it. Scroll while eating. Navigate slides from across the room. Pinch to click, swipe for arrows, speak to type. All processing runs locally — no cloud, no latency, no data leaving your machine.

Features

  • Cursor control — pinch your right hand and move to drag the cursor
  • Click & right-click — quick pinch for click, long pinch (1s) for right-click
  • Swipe navigation — open hand swipe for arrow keys (velocity-based, no false triggers)
  • Scroll — left pinch + right fist, then move left hand up/down
  • Drag and drop — pinch both hands, move right hand to drag
  • Speech-to-text — hold both fists to activate dictation
  • Escalating delete — hold 🤙 to delete chars, then words, then lines
  • Circle-to-screenshot — pinch and draw a circle to capture that region to clipboard
  • Voice commands — say "press down" or "command z" during speech mode
  • Dynamic Island — Liquid Glass overlay (macOS 26+) showing hands, gestures, and screenshot previews
  • Configurable — adjust FPS (30/60/90/120), cursor sensitivity, and scroll speed
  • Fully local — zero network dependency, everything on-device

Install

git clone https://github.com/TomYang-TZ/Gstrl.git
cd Gstrl
make install

Requires macOS 14+, a webcam, and Swift 5.9+. Grant Camera, Accessibility, Screen Recording, and Speech Recognition permissions when prompted (app opens settings pages automatically).

Use Cases

Quick capture — circle something on screen to screenshot it instantly.

Eating at your desk — scroll and navigate without touching your keyboard.

Presentations — control slides from across the room with natural gestures.

RSI relief — alternate between mouse and gestures to reduce strain.

Accessibility — another input channel for users with limited fine motor control.

Standing desk — step back from your desk and keep working.
